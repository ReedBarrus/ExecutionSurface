# DME Architect Morphogenesis Workflow Seed
## Status

This workflow governs early-stage architect-bundle development before DME has its own runtime support for process management.

It is intentionally simplified.

Current simplifications:

single active branch only
linear default flow
Administrator may reroute or repeat roles when needed
Auditor temporarily performs Gate function
Administrator temporarily performs Archivist function

This workflow exists to support controlled architectural generation without premature smoothing, uncontrolled divergence, or false release into deeper layers.

## Purpose

This workflow exists to:

generate architecture bundle candidates
preserve productive asymmetry long enough to test it
compress only what survives pressure
decide when a subject is ready to unfold into the next layer
keep deferred but valuable alternatives from being lost

It is not a general workflow for all DME development.
It is a targeted workflow for architect-bundle morphogenesis.

## Core Rule

Resolution is not allowed to erase distinctions that have not yet been tested for architectural load-bearing value.

### Secondary Rule

Expansion is only lawful when the next layer’s active subject is more specific than the current one, not merely more detailed.

## Active Roles
### 1. Administrator

Current holder: Reed

Function:

selects the active subject
selects the next role
maintains single-branch discipline
approves reroutes, repeats, escalation, or deferral
keeps the process bounded
carries deferred alternatives into archive notes

Administrator is the process glue.
Administrator is not required to produce content in every pass.

### 2. Constructor

Function:

generates or revises architecture structure
synthesizes candidate formulations
builds compact bundle surfaces
incorporates approved corrections

Bias:

structure generation
forward motion
candidate production

Failure mode:

premature architecture
overbuilding
smoothing by construction
### 3. Creative

Function:

introduces contrastive framings
stretches scope in a controlled way
proposes nearby alternate cuts
prevents premature closure
injects useful internal or external structural pressure when approved

Bias:

productive asymmetry
controlled divergence
attractor discovery

Failure mode:

scope bloom
novelty without integration
opening too many alternatives at once
### 4. Reflector

Function:

compresses without flattening
reconciles candidate structure into the smallest coherent form
preserves meaningful asymmetry while removing noise
checks whether terms belong where they are placed

Bias:

coherence
relation
functional compression

Failure mode:

premature resolution
beautiful blur
false unity
### 5. Auditor

Function:

checks claims against evidence and current workflow law
identifies hidden co-location, class drift, undefined terms, and false certainty
determines whether the current subject is ready to unfold
temporarily serves as Gatekeeper

Bias:

claim discipline
boundary integrity
reality pressure

Failure mode:

overblocking
excessive conservatism
failure to release lawful growth
## Default Linear Flow

Default order is:

Constructor
Creative
Reflector
Auditor

This is the default, not a prison.

Administrator may lawfully do:

Constructor → Constructor
Constructor → Reflector → Constructor
Constructor → Auditor
Creative → Reflector
Auditor → Constructor
Reflector → Auditor
or other bounded reroutes

So long as:

one active subject remains in scope
the reason for reroute is stated
no silent widening occurs

## Subject States

Allowed subject states:

Seeded
A usable candidate shape exists.
Tension-Held
Important asymmetry is active and should not yet be compressed away.
Compressed
A bounded synthesis exists.
Gate-Pending
The subject is ready for release review.
Released
The subject may unfold into the next layer.
Deferred
The subject is not rejected, but not active now.
Downgraded
The subject must return to a lower stabilizing layer.
Rebound
An attempted unfold exposed instability and the subject must step back.
Archived
The subject or alternative is inactive but retained for future reference.
## Pass Output Rule

Every pass must output all of the following:

active subject
active role
state before pass
state after pass
what changed
what remains unresolved
recommended next role
whether escalation or deferral is requested

If that output is missing, the pass is incomplete.

## Escalation Lanes

Each role may escalate only in bounded ways.

Constructor may escalate to:
Creative, when alternate framing is needed
Reflector, when overgrowth must be compressed
Auditor, when readiness or integrity must be tested
Creative may escalate to:
Reflector, when divergence has produced enough structure
Auditor, when a proposed contrast may violate current law
Administrator, when new input would widen scope too much
Reflector may escalate to:
Constructor, when compression reveals missing structure
Auditor, when a compressed form is ready for integrity check
Administrator, when two formulations cannot yet be lawfully merged
Auditor may escalate to:
Constructor, for correction
Creative, if the structure is too flattened and needs restored tension
Administrator, for defer / downgrade / archive / subject reset
## Deferral Pathways

Any role may request deferral, but only the Administrator may set a subject to Deferred or Archived.

Use Deferred when:

the idea is useful
it is not ready now
it should remain available later

Use Archived when:

the branch or alternative is inactive
it may still carry future value
it should not continue to consume active attention

Use Downgraded when:

the current subject is too unstable at its current layer
the next move must return to a more basic stabilizing surface

Use Rebound when:

an attempted unfold failed
the subject must step back without being discarded
## Auditor-as-Gate Rule

Until a separate Gate role exists, the Auditor performs gatekeeping.

The Auditor must not ask whether a subject is perfect.

The Auditor asks whether the current subject is stable enough to unfold one layer deeper without multiplying ambiguity.

## Minimum Gate Criteria

A subject may be Released only if:

its boundary is explicit
its non-claims are explicit
its unresolved tensions are named
its current form is compressive rather than bloated
the next deeper subject can be named clearly in one phrase

If any of those fail, the Auditor should recommend:

Defer
Downgrade
Rebound
or another role pass
Gate Output Format

When acting as gatekeeper, the Auditor should return:

decision: Release / Defer / Downgrade / Rebound / Hold
next layer: same / deeper / prior
next active subject: exact subject name
reason: one short explanation

Example:

decision: Release
next layer: deeper
next active subject: Commit-Boundary Contract Shape
reason: major partition and commit law are stable enough to unfold into explicit contract geometry
## Next-Subject Selection Rule

The next active subject must always be:

narrower than the current subject
more specific than the current subject
directly implied by the current subject’s unresolved tension or release point

Never unfold into “more detail everywhere.”

Always unfold into one named target.

## Archive Rule

Administrator keeps a lightweight archive of:

deferred alternatives
rejected but informative formulations
unresolved tensions that are not currently active
useful future tests or comparison ideas

Archive exists to preserve value without widening active scope.

## Single-Branch Rule

Only one active subject may be in motion at a time.

No parallel active branches unless the workflow is explicitly upgraded later.

Creative may introduce alternatives, but those alternatives must remain subordinate to the single active subject until the Administrator chooses otherwise.

## One-Line Summary

This workflow supports controlled architectural morphogenesis by using bounded role asymmetry, explicit subject states, and auditor-gated release to unfold one active subject at a time without premature smoothing or uncontrolled scope growth.

## Live Subject Surface And Write-Rights Rule

Architect morphogenesis must operate on a real live subject surface, not only on commentary about the subject.

For each active subject, maintain:

* one **live subject surface**
* one **cycle log**

### Live subject surface

The live subject surface is the current working document section for the active subject.

This is the text being actively shaped.

### Cycle log

The cycle log stores:

* administrator transfer notes
* accepted and rejected changes
* live tensions
* release / hold / defer decisions
* archived alternatives and deferred ideas

The cycle log is not the live subject surface.

## Default write-rights posture

During early single-branch morphogenesis:

* **Constructor** may directly revise the live subject surface
* **Reflector** may directly revise the live subject surface
* **Creative** may propose write payloads but does not directly mutate the live subject surface by default
* **Auditor** may propose correction targets, patch payloads, or release notes but does not directly mutate the live subject surface by default
* **Administrator** routes accepted payloads, tracks state, and decides what is promoted into the next writing pass

## Proposal-rights rule

All roles may return one or more of the following:

* replacement block
* insertion block
* deletion recommendation
* patch target
* wording correction
* explicit keep / split / defer recommendation

These are **write payload proposals**.

A proposal is not yet a live-surface mutation unless it is adopted in a writing-role pass or explicitly promoted by the Administrator.

## Mutation rule

Only one role may actively mutate the live subject surface in a given pass.

That mutation must be bounded to the active subject.

No role may silently mutate:

* neighboring subjects
* inactive sections
* the full architecture bundle at once

## Replace / Append / Log Rule

Use:

* **Replace**
  when refining an existing active subject section

* **Append**
  only when a subject has been lawfully released and a narrower next subject is being opened as a new section or new layer surface

* **Log only**
  for tensions, alternatives, audit findings, gate decisions, and deferred ideas that are not yet promoted into live subject text

## Role-specific writing behavior

### Constructor

Writes first-pass or corrective revisions into the live subject surface.

### Creative

Returns pressure payloads and alternate formulations.
These should normally go to the cycle log unless explicitly promoted.

### Reflector

May rewrite the live subject surface by compressing Constructor text under preserved Creative pressure.

### Auditor / Gate

Returns boundary findings, release decisions, and exact correction targets.
These should normally go to the cycle log unless explicitly promoted into a correction pass.

## Promotion rule

A proposal from Creative or Auditor may be promoted into the live subject surface only when:

* the Administrator explicitly accepts it
* it remains within the active subject
* it does not silently widen scope
* it is incorporated through a bounded writing pass

## Early-phase simplification

Until the workflow proves stable:

* keep one live subject surface only
* keep one active subject only
* prefer routed proposal rights over universal direct-write rights
* concentrate live-surface mutation in Constructor and Reflector

A broader write-rights model may be considered later if process stability is preserved.

## One-line summary

All roles may propose changes, but early live-document mutation stays concentrated in bounded writing passes so architect morphogenesis shapes a real subject surface without collapsing into uncontrolled multi-author drift.

## Delegated Process-Surface Update Rule

Early architect morphogenesis now delegates bounded process-surface mutation to the active roles.

### Live subject surface

* Constructor may directly write or revise the live subject surface.
* Reflector may directly rewrite or compress the live subject surface.
* Creative may propose patch payloads against the live subject surface but does not directly mutate it by default.
* Auditor may propose correction payloads against the live subject surface but does not directly mutate it by default.

### Cycle log

* Every role appends its own cycle-log entry at the end of its pass.
* The entry must use the cycle-log schema already established.
* If a role does not append its cycle-log entry, the pass is incomplete.

### Subject register

* Subject Register updates are performed only when subject state actually changes.
* Auditor-as-Gate updates the subject register when issuing:

  * Release
  * Hold
  * Defer
  * Downgrade
  * Rebound
  * Archive
* Constructor, Creative, and Reflector may recommend subject-register changes, but do not directly change subject state by default.

### State reality rule

A state transition is not fully active until it is written into the Subject Register.

A gate result may be treated as:

* **declared**
  when returned in the gate response

and

* **committed**
  when written into the Subject Register

### Administrator role

Administrator remains responsible for:

* selecting the next role
* preserving one active subject
* approving exceptions
* resolving conflicts between process surfaces
* maintaining archive/deferred notes

Administrator does not need to perform routine cycle-log or subject-register mutation unless a role fails to do so.

## One-line summary

Roles now mutate the process surfaces that belong to their function, while Administrator keeps routing and sovereignty without becoming the mechanical scribe for the whole loop.

## Metastability Rule

A subject may remain active as a **bounded leaf** when:

* it still carries real structural gain
* deeper descent would buy wording more than control
* upward compression would erase a still-live distinction

This state is called **Metastable Hold**.

### Metastable Hold

Metastable Hold means:

* the subject remains active
* no deeper child subject is opened
* no upward compression occurs yet
* the next pass must stabilize, tighten, or monitor the leaf rather than force motion

### Leaf-Stabilization Pass

When a subject is in Metastable Hold, the next role may perform a **Leaf-Stabilization Pass**.

A Leaf-Stabilization Pass may:

* tighten wording
* preserve live distinctions
* make the compression-up condition explicit
* reduce overstatement
* prepare the subject either for future compression upward or future reactivation

A Leaf-Stabilization Pass must not:

* open a new child subject
* force upward compression without evidence
* widen the subject into neighboring architecture

### Gate rule for metastability

When gating a subject, Auditor / Gate must consider three lawful outcomes:

* **Release downward** when a narrower child subject adds control
* **Compress upward** when the current distinction no longer earns its own depth
* **Metastable Hold** when the distinction still earns its own depth but further descent would produce diminishing-return fractalization

Structural-gain judgment: descend / hold as metastable leaf / compress upward

When a subject enters Metastable Hold, Administrator must either:

park it and activate a new subject,
or explicitly schedule a later re-open condition.

### One-line summary

A subject may lawfully remain as a metastable bounded leaf when it still carries structural gain but neither deeper descent nor upward compression is yet justified.
